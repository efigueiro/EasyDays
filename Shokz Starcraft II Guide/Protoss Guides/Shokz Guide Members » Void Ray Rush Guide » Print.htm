Void Ray Rush Guide

Posted By Shokz On May 18, 2010 @ 5:11 pm In Protoss Guides | No Comments

The Void Ray is a powerful unit that you can get very quickly in the game. In most games if you’re able to quickly tech to void rays the chances of your opponent having enough anti-air to defend you is slim, although this is all based on having a good timing build. Once you have a Void Ray you must know how to micro the Void Ray so that you can stay alive as you continue to macro and send in additional Void Rays or reinforcements.

Void Ray Rush Build Order

In order to be successful at a Void Ray rush you need to get the Void Rays out in the shortest amount of time possible, this is where practice and knowing the build order will help.

10/10 supply - Build a Pylon

10/18  supply -- Build a Gateway

10/18 supply -- Build an Assimilator, as soon as the Assimilator is finished put 3 Probes harvesting Gas

10/18 supply -- Send a Probe to scout unit’s location if on 2v2 Map.

13/18 supply -- As soon as the Gateway is finished make Cybernetics Core

14/18 supply -- As soon as Cybernetics Core is finished build Starport

Once your Starport is finished, immediately start building your first Void Ray; set the rally point  to your opponent’s base. Make sure that you use Chrono Boost on your Starport while your Void Ray is in production. If you are looking to take out their main base, then you will want to wait till you get 2 — 3 Void Rays so that you can fly right into your opponents base, and take it out.

Keeping it hidden

The key to a successful void ray rush is to not let your opponent know that you are quickly teching to Void Rays, as a good player will be able to easily counter the rush if they know it’s coming.

When are building for a Void Ray rush it is a good idea to wall off your base so your opponent cannot get in and scout. When you are building for a Void Ray rush, it is a good idea to wall off your base, making it impossible for your opponent to get in and scout. You can do this by walling off your entrance with 2 Gateways and then upgrading them to Warp gates later in the game to make ground units, or do the more traditional Protoss wall off, which can be found in our Protoss Wall off guide.

However,  make sure you get a stalker or two  when you wall off you so they can shoot over your ramp wall and defend against any early rushes.

Proxy Stargate

Another strategy to use when doing a Void Ray rush is to build a proxy pylon  and Stargate outside of your base closer to your opponents base, but hidden in a corner. The reason for this is that if he scouts you, he will not see your Stargate and will not expect the Void Ray rush. It will also  shorten the travel time from your Stargate to your opponents base.
When doing this you want to build your base normally, because if/when your opponent scouts you, if he were to see your base built up unusually, then he might expect a proxy base which would lead to him scouting the map for one…so try to keep to your normal base build and macro.
