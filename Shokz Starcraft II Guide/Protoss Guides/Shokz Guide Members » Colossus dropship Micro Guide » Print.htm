Colossus dropship Micro Guide

Posted By Shokz On May 21, 2010 @ 4:58 pm In Protoss Guides | No Comments

The Colossus is a great Protoss unit that is generally the backup force of your army and will stand behind your zealots, stalkers and immortals, but when you want to get creative your able to be very effective with a Colossus dropship harass either on the opponents army or on their minerals lines.

Getting Prepared

To do the Colossus dropship harass you are going to need Colossus of course, and a Warp Prism, both of these are built from a Robotics Bay so I generally will get atleast 2 Robotics bays and Chrono boost both the Colossus and Warp Prism to get them out ASAP. Once you have your Colossus and Warp Prism make sure to hot key them both so you can quickly access them while in battle or when you are harassing.

Note: Research the Colossus Range Upgrade

This Colossus harass is not going to be enough to destroy your enemies base, but if done correctly it can turn the game in your favor so you still need to produce units. Since you already have a Robotics Bay I would go with a heavy Stalker, Immortal build with a few Colossus to to back them up. That way once your done harassing them you’ll be able to finish them up with your actual army.

Colossus Harassment

Once you are ready load a Colossus into your Warp Prism and make your way to the back of your opponents base, make sure you have a clear path to the base since Warp Prism go down easily when under fire. Unload your Colossus behind the mineral line and let him go to town on the workers, once you see your opponent start to bring his units to defend his minerals load your Colossus in your Warp Prism and fly out. Continue to do do this while you build a decent attack force in your base.

Once you are ready to attack take your army to his base and engage him, he will bring all his forces to defend against your attack and this is when you can go drop your Colossus behind his minerals once again. Most players will focus on the main attack in the front of his base and you’ll be able to take out all his workers and possibly even his main base.

Using the High Ground

On a lot of maps your minerals are next to a high or low ground where you can move your Colossus up and down from, if the map your own has this use this to your advantage when attacking and micro your Colossus up and down off the cliff keeping him out of range of attack while keeping the pressure on him.
