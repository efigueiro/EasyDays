Zergling Guide

Posted By Shokz On July 21, 2010 @ 6:36 am In Zerg Unit Guides | Comments Disabled

Race Weak Against Okay Against Strong Against
Zerg Brood Lord, Mutalisk, Ultralisk, Baneling, Roach Hydralisk
Protoss Carrier, Mothership, Void Ray, Archon, Colossus, Dark Templar, Phoenix, Zealot High Templar, Sentry, Immortal, Stalker
Terran Banshee, Battlecruiser, Hellion, Reaper Ghost, Marine, Siege Tank, Viking Marauder, Thor

Zerglings are small, fast, and quick units which work well in the masses, these units are the first Zerg attacking unit your able to build and with good Micro they can be used through out the entire game depending on your opponents build. The new pathing system also makes Zerglings much more aggressive in the heat of battle because they surround their enemy much more easily now. The downside to this is that units bunch together now, making Zerglings much more susceptible to Siege Tank fire and other splash attacks.

Skills and Strategies

Metabolic Boost: Increases the movement speed of all Zerglings by 20%, this is a must have upgrade if you plan on using a lot of Zerglings, once you have this upgrade Zerglings are now known as Speedlings since they have the speed upgrade. Once you have the speed upgrade Zerglings become the fastest units in the game and are able to quickly race across the map and harass your opponent, this works great in early game to run your Speedlings right past your opponents units into their mineral line and start taking out their gatherers.

Adrenal Glands: Increases the attack speed of all Zerglings by 20%, although requires a Hive. This is a great upgrade also but unlike Metabolic Boost where you can research right away, Adrenal Glands you must have a Hive before you can research this upgrade making it not available till late game. A lot of times if games go into the late game this is a good upgrade to get if you have the extra cash because you never know when you may need to pop out a bunch of Zerglings to counter a unit such as mass Thors in the late game.

  • Speedling Rush Guide
  • Run your Zerglings past your opponents army into their minerals to harass their economy.
  • Use Zerglings to run around the map and check for opponent expansions.
  • Zerglings can be used through out the entire game, don’t just think you can only use them to rush

Zergling/Hydralisk Combination:
The Zergling and Hydralisk are a force to be reckoned with. The Zergling’s main role in this type of attack is the primary fighting force against any ground units. Even though the Hydralisks are there for support, the Zerglings are also there for protecting the Hydralisks. Obviously the Zerglings should be in the lead of this attack.

Zergling/Mutalisk Combination:
This is a much more versatile approach than the Hydralisks. The Mutalisks have more speed than the Hydralisks, and the Zerglings have much more space to move about. The Zerglings main role is attacking the ground units again, but there are different variations that can occur, since the Mutalisk is an air unit.

Against other Races:

Vs Protoss: If you rush a Protoss player with Zerglings and they don’t wall off their base with a Gateway / Zealot then you should try to run right past their entrance and into their mineral field, if your able to get back there and start taking out Probes then you will be in great shape and can try to continue to send more units and win the game. Zerglings are very strong against stalkers if the can surround them which will allow you to quickly take out his army in a rush if he teched straight to Stalkers.

Vs Terran: It is almost impossible to Zerling rush Terran players since they almost always / should wall off their base with a Barracks and Supply Depot. The one thing you can do to get past the wall off is to use Banelings to break down the wall and then run into his base with your Zerglings, for more info check out the Baneling Bust Guide. Although outside of the Terran’s base Zerglings are a strong counter to mass Marauders and are great against Thors, if you can get a surround on a Thor you can take him down extremely quickly.

Vs Zerg: Zergling vs Zergling rush matches are fun as it comes down to who can micro the best, if you are stuck in a Zergling rush match with another Zerg player a good idea would be to get a couple Banelings, if you can get them to explode on their Zerglings you can easily overwhelm him and usually take him out. The main thing you need to watch out for is a tech to Roaches because as soon as your opponent get’s roaches you need to fall out of Zerglings and start going a different route such as Roaches yourself or Hydras into Mutalisks.
