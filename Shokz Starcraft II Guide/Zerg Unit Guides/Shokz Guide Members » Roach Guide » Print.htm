Roach Guide

Posted By Shokz On July 22, 2010 @ 5:33 am In Zerg Unit Guides | Comments Disabled

Race Weak Against Okay Against Strong Against
Zerg Brood Lord, Mutalisk Baneling, Hydralisk, Ultralisk Zergling
Protoss Carrier, Immortal, Mothership, Void Ray, Colossus, Phoenix, Stalker Archon, Dark Templar, High Templar, Sentry Zealot
Terran Banshee, Battlecruiser, Marauder, Siege Tank Ghost Hellion, Marine, Reaper, Thor, Viking

Roach is strong armored early game unit which can be very hard to deal with in quick rushes. These powerful units are hard to take out, but their extra armor can be a downfall if you go against units such as Immortals that do extra damage against armored units. In most games roaches are great to mix with Zerglings or Hydralisks as they provide a good balance with both mixes and work well with almost any combination that your opponent will have in a early to mid game attack.

Skills and Strategies

Tunneling Claws: Trained from the Roach Warren it enables the Roach to move while burrowed. While burrowed, the Roach also has increased health regeneration. This can be used to tunnel your roaches underground to your opponents minerals pop up and take out his workers, although this becomes more difficult as you get higher up in leagues. The Ability to burrow though is a great way to flee from a battle because not only will you be able to get your roaches away from a losing battle with out being killed you will regen health while doing so.

Glial Reconstitution: This skill is also trained from the Roach Warren and it increases movement speed of your roaches. I don’t find my self using this to often since if I need upgraded speed for my units I will get it for my Zerglings or I use Creep Tumors to spreed creep across the map which increases the speed of all my units. If you plan on going an all in Roach Attack then use this upgrade will he helpful since it will allow your reinforcement Roaches to get to your army quicker.

Roach/Zergling Combination
This is the earliest possible combination you can have in a game. The main idea for this is be treated as a watered down version of the Ultralisk/Zergling combination. The Roach’s role is lead the force head on and take most of the damage first, then let the Zerglings charge in. In this case, the Roaches should try to create a distraction, because they can absorb most of the damage by using their fast regeneration.

Against other Races

Vs Protoss: If your Protoss opponent is going for a loaded Zealot rush then Roaches are what you want! I would skip Zerglings all together when you see a Zealot rush and go straight for Roaches since they can counter them very easily, later in the rush if you see stalkers starting to appear then you can mix in a couple Zerglings and your Roach / Zergling build should be good, although once your opponent starts getting Immortals you need to transition out of roaches as Immortals do ridiculous damage against them.

Vs Terran: Roaches are great against most of the Terran early units such as Marines, Repaers and Hellions although can be taken out quickly with Marauders and even quicker if the Marauders have Stim pack. If you see your opponent is massing marauders you can keep making a few Roaches, but get a Zergling heavy Roach / Zergling army and you will counter his mass Marauders. The same goes for Siege Tanks, they can take out a group of Roaches fast so building Zerlings to take out the Siege Tanks or Mutaliskks will be the best way to go.

Vs Zerg: Just like with Zergling battles Roach wars will turn into who can produce the most and micro the best, or transition out of them, if your opponent continues to produce roaches you can slowly tech into Mutalisks which will give you the Arial advantage, just make sure you continue to produce enough roaches to defend your base or put enough Spine Crawlers down in defense so you can hold off any attack till you get Mutalisks.
