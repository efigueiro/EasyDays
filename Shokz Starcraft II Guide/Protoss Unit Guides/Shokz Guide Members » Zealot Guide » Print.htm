Zealot Guide

Posted By Shokz On July 17, 2010 @ 6:59 pm In Protoss unit Guides | No Comments

Race Weak Against Okay Against Strong Against
Zerg Boord Lord, Mutalisk, Ultralisk, Benling, Roach Hydralisk, Infestor, Queen Zerglings
Protoss Carrier, Mothership, Void Ray, Archon, Colossus, Dark Templar Sentry, Stalker High Templar, Immortal
Terran Battlecruiser, Banshee, Hellion, Reaper Ghost, Marauder, Siege Tank, Viking Marine, Thor

Zealots are the basic unit for Protoss, and is the strongest of the starting units for all three races. They are a strong unit in the start of the game although as the game progresses they are not as good, but with the upgraded Charge ability this makes them useful in certain strategies in the late game.

Zealots are most commonly known for Zealot rushes where you build 2 Gateways and Chrono Boost Zealots to quickly rush your opponent. This is a very strong move if the player is not expecting an early rush and works very well in the lower level ladder leagues. A lot of players like to use a Zealot rush to try to throw their opponent off their Macro game and slow them up, they key here is making a couple Zealots around 2 – 3 and have them sent right to your opponents base as they are made. This requires good micro and macro as your goal is to slow your opponent down keeping him focused on the small attack while you continue to build into your main strategy with out allowing your attack to slow you down.

Skills and Strategies

Charge: Allows the Zealot to intercept nearby enemies by increasing their movement speed as they charge in. This skill is auto casted and can be used every 10 seconds and is very useful in the late game to quickly close the gap between Zealots and ranged units.

Against other Races

Vs Protoss: A Zealot rush in Protoss vs Protoss is a very strong strategy, since the main factor is who can make more Zealots faster, and if they are not ready for a Zealot rush then you will have the upper hand on the rush. You only want to do a Zealot rush on maps that don’t have a really long rush distance between bases, for example Desert Oasis would be a very bad map to do a Zealot rush on since the distance between bases is very long, but you could always do a Proxy Zealot rush which will be discussed in detail in our Protoss Proxy guide coming soon.

Vs Terran: Zealots are not the strongest when facing Terran, it is hard to rush them since most Terran will wall off their base and if you try to break down their wall they will just bring a couple SCVs over to repair the wall while a Marine or Marauder shoots at your Zealots, in this case you would want to wait till mid game and see what the Terran player is going, if he is going a heavy Mech build with Thors and Tanks then Zealots with Charge would would great.

Vs Zerg: Zealots are very strong against Zerglings as long as they don’t surround you and with a mass amount of numbers, a Zealot rush works well against a macro Zerg player who is looking to do an early expansion. This is where scouting is very crucial, if you see the Zerg is going for an early expansion then you should be able to do a good amount of harassment with early Zealots if not win the game. Later on in the game Zealots with Charge is a great counter to Hydralisks along with Immortals an Stalkers mixed in your army.
