Void Ray Guide

Posted By Shokz On July 20, 2010 @ 5:33 am In Protoss unit Guides | No Comments

Race Weak Against Okay Against Strong Against
Zerg Corruptor, Hydralisk Mutalisk, Queen Brood Lord, Benling, Infestor, Overseer, Roach, Ultralisk, Zergling
Protoss Stalker, Archon, Phoenix Carrier, Sentry Colossus, Dark Templar, High Templar, Immortal, Mothership, Zealot
Terran Marine, Viking Battlecrusier, Ghost Banshee, Hellion, Marauder, Reaper, Siege Tank, Thor

Void Rays are one of the Protoss strongest and most common air units. The Void Ray has 3 levels of damage and it increases the longer the Void Ray remains attacking a single target, if the Void Ray switches targets quickly while at the max damage output it will continue to output the maximum damage.

Void Rays have been known to be a rushing unit with Protoss players teching straight to Void Rays and if your opponent is unprepared then the strategy can work although if scouted good players will be able to quickly counter them. If you plan on doing a Void Ray rush either block off the entrance of your base preventing scouting or hide your Stargate in the corner of your base or in the corner of the map.

  • Void Ray Rush Guide
  • When doing a Void Ray rush go straight for their main base (Nexus, Hatchery, Command Center)

Against other Races

Vs Protoss: Void Rays are tough against Protoss, Stalkers are a great counter to Void Rays and are a normal part in most build orders, I do like to use a couple Void Rays while playing against another Protoss player to harass their expansions. Using Void Rays to hit their workers and then run away once their army comes works well and this also allows you to get their army out position in order attack with your main force.

Vs Terran: A rush to Void Rays works well against a Terran player as long as they don’t mass marines, most Terran players like to go a heavy Marauder build into Siege Tanks or Banshees which allow your Void Rays to excel. The two things you have to watch out for are mass Marines with stim pack and Vikings both do very well against

Vs Zerg: Zerg does not have any real anti-air besides their queen until they upgrade to a Liar and get Hydralisks. Void Rays can be very effective if you can get them out before the Zerg player has a chance to go Hydralisks or Mutalisks. Void Rays can still be effective if your opponent goes a Hydralisk by going Zealots with the Charge ability and the Void Rays backing them up.
