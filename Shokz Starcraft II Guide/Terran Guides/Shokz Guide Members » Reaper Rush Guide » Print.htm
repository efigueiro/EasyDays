Reaper Rush Guide

Posted By Shokz On June 12, 2010 @ 11:29 pm In Terran Guides | No Comments

Reapers are a light weight Terran unit which has the ability to jump up and down from cliffs. This gives the reaper the unique ability to quickly travel anywhere on the map quickly as they don’t have to follow the normal path. Reapers are commonly used as an early game rush unit to harass your opponents minerals and destroy their economy giving your the early game advantage.

Reapers as a stand alone unit are generally very weak and you won’t see them in an army composition as they have light armor, low hp, and die very easily. Although if you know how to use them correctly they can excel and be one of the most beneficial units through out the game.  We are going to look at the build orders for reaper rushes, how to use them during the rush and how they can still be used mid to late game.

Rush Preparation

When doing a Reaper rush you want to get the Reaper out as fast as possible so that you are in your opponents base before he has any units to defend.

Scouting – When Reaper Rushing make sure you scout your opponent in the very early game with an SCV so you know his location. Once your Reaper is ready you want to be able to go straight to their base and not worry about finding where your opponent is on the map. If your playing a map with only two spawn locations then you will know where he is although if your on a map with more than 2 spawn points then you will need to scout early game.

Base Build – When going the Reaper rush build make sure you build your Barracks and Supply Depot next to your ramp to wall of your base. If your Reaper rush fails you and your opponent counters having your base walled off will allow you to defend against the counter.

The Build Order

9 – Barracks

Once you reach 9 supply save 150 minerals and build a barracks, as soon as the barracks is complete build a Tech Lab onto the barracks

9 – Refinery

Once you start building your Barracks save 75 minerals and build a refinery while still at 9 supply. You will need enough gas for a Tech lab once your barracks is complete along with gas for your reapers so put 3 SCVs mining gas once the refinery is complete

10 – Supply Depot

You want to build your first supply depot at 10 or 11 supply the key is having your first Supply Depot done before the Tech Lab is finished so you can instantly start building your first Reaper.

Controlling the Reapers

Once your Barracks is complete and you start building your first Reaper set your rally point of your Barracks to the outside of your opponents base that so your Reaper will start traveling there right away. Once your first Reaper is created set him to a Control Group such as 1 so you can quickly access him. You will also want to set a hot key your Barracks so you can access it and continue to make Reapers while Microing your first Reaper in your opponents base.

Back of the Base - When you are ready to attack you want to go in through the side of your opponents base where you think he will not have any units so you can quickly get to his minerals. Stay along the wall and out of sight of where he would place any units and go straight behind his mineral line and focus fire his workers.

Group of Reapers – A lot of times I like to wait till I get at least 3 – 4 Reapers before I go into their base that way I can one shot their Mineral workers and take a lot of very quickly. Another reason for waiting until you get 3 – 4 Reapers is that once he sees you have gone a Reaper rush your opponent will build to counter it and within a minute he will have the correct counter and 1 reaper usually won’t be enough to take him out. Although if you have 3 – 4 Reapers you’ll be able to enough damage before he can counter.

Reaper Kite - Reapers are very fast and if Micro correctly you can kite your opponent’s early game units. For example if your playing a Protoss you can kite a Zealot by running the Reaper a couple yards away and then shooting at the Zealot and then running away again, continue to this process without letting the Zealot catch the Reaper.

Using the Cliffs - When attacking your opponents base you want to make sure you use your cliffs to your advantage. If the back of your opponents mineral line is near a cliff jump up and attack his workers, when he brings his units then jump back down and then enter his base from a different location, to draw his units to the new spot and then jump back down and head back to his mineral line. By doing this your keeping pressure on him while hopefully damaging his economy and killing his mineral workers.

Reapers Mid to Late Game

Rapers are mainly used for early game rush but they can also be used during the mid to late game. Now they are not a unit that you want to use in battle mid to late game but can be used as a distraction and to help prevent your opponent from expanding.

The key is keeping your Reapers separated from your army and keeping your opponent on the move. If your opponent is expanding somewhere on the map you can use your Reapers to attack his expansion and when he brings his army to attack you can then move in on his base with your main force.

Another key strategy is to wait till you get into a battle with your opponent and then take your Reapers into his base and take out his workers on the Mineral line. Since your opponents army is currently in battle with you he won’t be able to defend his units and most players will focus on the battle rather then their base. They key to this is having your Rapers hot keyed so you can quickly select them and send them right into your opponents base while in the middle of a battle.

Transition into Macro

They purpose of the Reaper rush is hurt your opponents economy and get them out of their normal play build. Although you also need to be able to transition into a mid game since it is rare that you’ll win the game with Reapers alone. Make sure before you attack with your Reapers you have your Command Center hot keyed so you can continue to produce SCVs and new units.

Make sure that during the attack you continue to build units such as Marauders or start building a Factory / Starport depending on what you would like to transition into. The key is having units being built and keep up with your Macro while attacking. The worst thing you can do attack and let your minerals build up so that once your attack is finished you have 1000+ minerals and no units.
