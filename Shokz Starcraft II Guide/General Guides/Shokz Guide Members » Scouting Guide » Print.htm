Scouting Guide

Posted By Shokz On May 27, 2010 @ 9:09 pm In General Guides | No Comments

Scouting is one of the most important things you can do in the game as it gives you information on your enemies build and what units they are getting allowing you to build the units to counter him.

In every game you should send one of your works such as an SCV, Probe, or Drone to scout your enemy’s base early game. You need to send them out around 9 or 10 supply and hot key the scout so you may micro him while still managing your base. I usually like to set my scout to Control Group #1 so while I’m still building my base I can quickly access him while I have him dance around the enemies base keeping him on the move and not letting him die.

The reason why you want to send a scout out so early is for two reasons. One you need to find out where the players base is on a map if the map has more than 2 spawning locations. Second you want to make sure they are not doing anything Cheesy. If you head into their base and you see that a Protoss doesn’t have a Gateway being built or that a Terran isn’t building a Barracks then you know they may be trying to proxy you and you need to then scout all around your base and get ready to defend against a cheese.

Terran Scouting

Besides sending a SCV to scout early game you can also build a barracks next to your opponents base and fly it over their base as a scout. This method is usually done mid game and is used instead of using a Scan on your opponent as you should be using a Mule instead since a Mule will collect 260+ minerals and a Barracks is only 150 minerals

Protoss Scouting

As Protoss besides scouting early game with your probe you’ll have a few options to keep an eye on your opponent. The first and most obvious is creating a observer from your robotics bay. This is ideally the best way to go as they are invisible and you can keep an eye on your opponent’s base as long as he doesn’t have any detectors.

If your opponent does have detectors and your not able to get an observer into his base then create a Phoenix and use the hallucination ability from a sentry to create a copy of the Phoenix and fly it over your enemies base. The great thing about this method is that even of the hallucinated phoenix dies you didn’t waste any resources as it was created with just energy from your Sentry.

Zerg Scouting

Zerg has the ability to scout with overlords although early game it’s not always the best to send an overlord right into your opponents base. Overlords with out the speed upgrade from the Liar are generally very slow and 1 marine, stalker, or queen can easily dispatch of an overlord inside their base which can harm you early game and supply block you. This is why Zerg players usually use a drone to scout instead of an overlord early in the game while sending their overlords to cliffs in the middle of the map to give them sight of pathways to their base.

Once you get a Lair and get a the Overlord speed upgrade then it can be good idea to sacrifice an Overlord and fly into their opponents base to see what they are building. Some times you may be able to fly the overlord through their base to scout and have him make it out alive which you can then heal him with your queen.
