Speedling Rush Guide

Posted By Shokz On June 15, 2010 @ 4:03 pm In Zerg Guides | No Comments

Zerglings with the speed upgrade “Metabolic Boost” are commonly referred to as Speedlings. They are a unit which I use every time I play as Zerg and they are effective at all levels of the game and are one of the funniest units to play with.

The reason for going Speedlings every game I play Zerg is they are a great to defend your base and can be used to harass your opponents minerals giving your the economic edge and in most cases win you the game.

Speedling Build Order

10 – Spawning Pool

10 – Extractor

10 – Overlord

13 – Queen

When start the game you want to build drones till you reach 10 supply, once your at 10 supply save 200 minerals for a Spawning Pool. Once you started the Spawning pool you need to start an extractor right away and place 3 drones on gas once its finished becaues you will need 100 gas for the Metabolic Boost once your spawning pool is finished.

Right when your Spawning Pool finishes build a queen and then get the Metabolic Boost as soon as you have 100 Minerals & 100 Gas.

Preparing the Attack

Keep up Production – Before you attack make sure you hot key your hatchery hot keyed so you can continue to produce Zerglings during the attack along with your Queen so you can cast “Spawn Larva” on your hatchery to keep your unit production high.

Split Groups – I always split my Zerglings into two control groups which will allow me to micro them easier if I want to do a split attack or use one group as a diversion which I will explain shortly.

General Attack Strategies

When you are ready to attack take your units to the entrance of your opponents base, than take a single Zergling and run it into their base to see layout and the units they have. How you attack is going to depend on if you think you can defeat their army or if you just want to harass their minerals.

Attacking the Army - If they have a small army and you can take them out with your Zerglings then run unto their base and fight them in the open so you can gain a surround on their units. Make sure to fight them away from any static defense they have and way from any choke points so that your Zerglings can get a surround on them.

Going for the Minerals – If your opponent built for a rush and has a good size army which your Zerglings wouldn’t be able to take out then you need to focus on causing economic damage. Try to draw your opponents army out of their base, than run past them right into their mineral lines and let your Speedlings start attacking their workers. Once he brings his forces as backup run your Speedlings out of their and run them around his base keeping them away from his army and then dash back into on the minerals.

Split Attack - Before I attack I always split my Speedlings into 3 Control Groups, 1 group selects all the Speedlings, the 2nd selects 1 half while the 3rd control group selects the second half of the Speedlings. The Reason I do this is that it will allow me to easily surround my opponents forces since I can have one group hit from the front and then flank the other group around and attack from the back.

You can also use the two groups as a diversion, if your opponent is sitting on his ramp you can use one group to draw his forces out of his base while you take your other group and run them into the back of his base to attack his resources.

Chasing them down - Speedlings are the fastest ground units in Starcraft 2, you can use this to your advantage to chase down scouts, or units retreating from a failed attack. When chasing down a unit run your Speedlings in front of the unit so they can then surround them and stop their movement. You don’t want to just chase the unit attacking from behind because the unit will continue to move compared to if you surround him he won’t be able to keep moving and go down quickly.

Attacking Protoss

A lot of Protoss players will not wall off their base and if they do they will not do so correctly, this will allow you to run right past any zealots or stalkers they have into their mineral line and start taking out workers. This should be your first priority when hitting Protoss players.

If you see that you heavily out number the Protoss Units you should go directly at them and take them out. You want to get a surround on zealots and stalkers to take them out quickly and not let them get cornered against a wall or building where you cannot surround them so don’t engage until they are out in the open.

Once you’ve killed his units talk a look at how his gateways are setup, if there is only one pylon or two powering the gateways try to take them out first. If your able to powerdown his gateways you’ve pretty much won the game as long as you keep producing Speedlings and sending them into his base to finish off his probes.

Attacking Terran

Terran almost always wall off their base and put a few marines or marauders behind their wall, this makes it almost impossible to break through the wall with just Speedlings.

When a you see that Terran is walling off their base you have two options, one is to go for a Banling wall break and then run your speedlings into his base which is discussed in further detail in our Banling Terran Wall Break Guide. Or you can quickly create a few roaches and use them to focus fire down a Supply Depot in the Terran wall and then run your zerglings into the Terran base.

Attacking Zerg

Speedling rushes against Zerg players is an interesting match up. How you play this will be dependent on if your opponent is going Speedlings as well or if they go for a tech based build and get Roaches and Hydras. In all situations when going against Zerg you should try to surround your opponents Queen and take her out first since she is the key to their economy. By doing this it will slow down their unit production since they won’t be able to cast “Spawn Larva” until they produce a new Queen.

Against Speedlings

If your opponent goes Speedlings you need out number him by producing more Speedlings then he has. Do not fight him next to his hatchery since he will have his queen as backup although if your able to quickly run in and take out his Queen then do so. This will allow you to produce more Speedlings at a faster rate if your able to take out his queen.

If he is doing an all in Speedling rush then build a Banelings nest and produce a few Banelings. Then when you attack bring in your Banelings in and you’ll be able to take out a large amount of his Speedlings with just a few Banelings giving you the advantage.

Against Macro Zerg

When a Zerg player is looking to Macro they will usually expand early with a 15 or 18 hatchery and go into a Roach or Hydra build. When they do this they will get a few Zerglings out to protect their base and maybe put some pressure on you, but by going straight Speedlings as long as you don’t wait to long to attack you should be able to take them out easily.

The key is to attack before they can build an army of Roaches or Hydralisk. A lot of players will build a spine crawler in their base for defense as they try to macro although you can pull his units out of his base and fight them out of range of his defense once you take them out you’ll be able to move in on his defense and queen.

Transition out of Speedlings

There are times when your Speedlings just didn’t work or you were able to do economic damage to your opponent, but need higher tier units to finish them off. Knowing how to transition into other units from a Speedling Rush is very important and doing so will help you finish off the game after your initial rush.

Start during the Rush – When you are rushing with your Speedlings and you see that you have extra resources use this to upgrade your Hatchery into a Lair. This is very important because a Liar is needed to create Hydralisk and Mutalisk which is two of the common transitional units from Speedlings . If you wait until after the rush to upgrade your Hatchery you will be stuck siting around waiting for your Liar to finish instead of producing new units. A lot of times I’ll even upgrade my Hatchery into Liar right before I go in for the first attack so by the time I”m done attacking I can then move on to tier 2 units.

Hydras – Producing Hydras after Speedlings is a normal transition and the one I use most often, the Hydras provide good anti air along with great ground support and with a group of Speedlings can quickly dispatch most builds.

Mutalisks – If you notice during the rush your opponent doesn’t have much anti air or isn’t close to getting any then you want to quickly build a Spire and start producing Mutaslisks . If you can get a couple of Mutalisks out before your opponent has any real anit air you’ll be able to pick off their workers and destroy their economy.
